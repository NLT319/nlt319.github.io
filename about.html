About

I'm a mechanical engineer with a BS and MS from UC Santa Barbara, focused on robotics, controls, and mechanical design. The problems I enjoy most are the ones where the mechanism and the controller have to be designed together, as the right hardware often makes the control problem much easier.

Experience

Hawkes Lab, UC Santa Barbara · 2022–2026

For my master's project I designed and built Cooper, a jumping robot that reorients itself in midair with a reaction wheel, covering the mechanical structure, BLDC actuation, and the embedded estimation and control running on an ESP32. Earlier in the lab I redesigned the energy transmission on a separate inertial jumping robot, improving its jump efficiency by 75%, and contributed structural and mechanical design to the lab's vine robot platforms, work that led to coauthorship on papers in IEEE RA-L and RAM.

CelLink · Summer 2025

As an equipment engineering intern I designed a tool-change mechanism for a robotic circuit assembler that cut cycle time by 70%, taking it from concept through machined and tested prototype in a high-volume manufacturing environment.

Analog Devices · Summer 2023

At their Beaverton wafer fab I led an overhaul of the facility's gas supply monitoring network, reprogramming the PLC controllers and designing replacement displays that alert technicians automatically when cylinder pressure runs low. The upgrades cut about 70 hours of manual supply rounds per week.
