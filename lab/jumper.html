Stick the landing

This is a cartoon of Cooper, a jumping robot I built that reorients itself in midair with a reaction wheel. It launches with a random tumble and needs to arrive aligned with the dashed landing line, foot first, so the leg can recoup the jump energy, which is where the name comes from. Land within about eight degrees of the line, and slowly, to stick it, and if it keeps crashing, the show me button toggles control over to the controller until you press it again.

How it works

The body and its reaction wheel exchange angular momentum and nothing else, so torquing the wheel one way spins the body the other, and the bar at the top shows how close the wheel is to saturation, past which you have no authority left in that direction. The body leaves the ground aligned with its takeoff velocity, and since the ballistic arc is symmetric, the landing line is the takeoff line mirrored about vertical. The show me toggle runs a saturated PD controller on attitude error, which is close in shape to what the real robot runs on its ESP32, and it stays engaged across rounds until toggled off, meaning that you can also flip it mid-flight to watch a recovery from whatever situation you got yourself into.
