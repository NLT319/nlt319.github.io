Suspension tuner

A car drives over a road that never repeats, and each corner of its suspension is a controller you tune live with the sliders while it drives. Kp acts like a spring, Kd like a damper, and the harshness number is the running RMS of vertical body acceleration, so soft and well damped beats stiff.

harshness: – m/s²

How it works

The car is a half-car model with heave and pitch, and each corner carries half the static weight as preload with a PD controller acting on top of it, trying to hold that corner of the body on the dashed target line above the road. Too little Kp lets the body follow every rise and dip of the terrain, too little Kd lets it wallow after each bump, and too much Kd feeds the road texture straight into the chassis. The terrain itself is a handful of sine waves whose amplitudes and frequencies wander on a mean-reverting random walk, with the phase integrated so frequency changes never cause jumps in the surface.
