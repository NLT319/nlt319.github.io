Cooper, a midair reorienting jumper

Hawkes Lab, MS project · 2025–2026

Cooper is a jumping robot that reorients itself in flight using a reaction wheel, as landing aligned lets it recoup the jump energy through the leg, which is where the name comes from. I designed and assembled the full system, covering the mechanical structure, BLDC actuation, IMU integration, and the embedded electronics, then developed a reduced order dynamics model and compared control strategies in MATLAB, including standard LQR, a braking wheel approach, and an optimized trajectory T-LQR. The best of them runs in C++ on an ESP32, estimating and correcting attitude in real time during ballistic flight on a spring loaded launcher, with telemetry streamed over WiFi for debugging. In testing, the system reliably cancelled rotation once aligned, though gyro drift left some persistent error in the attitude estimate, which further tuning in simulation is meant to address. Code is on GitHub.

Vine robots

Hawkes Lab · 2023–2025

I contributed structural and mechanical design to the lab's vine robot platforms across two projects. For the large industrial inspection robot, I designed the sensor tip mount that prevents the body material from jamming during eversion, along with the lightweight frame and electronics mounting, and built an automated inflatable column buckling test setup, which helped establish two of the robot's control parameters: the required growth pressure and the minimum retraction pressure. For the millimeter scale rapid vine robots, I designed a latched door test system based on the spring loaded mechanisms found in jellyfish stingers, and collected steady state velocity data as functions of driving pressure, payload mass, and scaled vine size, which the team used to fit coefficients for our dynamics model. This work led to coauthorship on two papers: "High-Velocity, Pressure-Driven Eversion for Rapid Vine Robots" in IEEE RA-L, and "Large-Scale Vine Robots for Industrial Inspection" in IEEE RAM. The test rigs and tip mount prototypes were machined on the lab's Tormach CNC mill and manual lathe.

Cart-pendulum stabilization

Control systems lab · 2025

Identified the plant with both parametric and nonparametric methods, doing the identification with the pendulum hanging down, as that configuration is stable enough to test directly, and flipping the sign on gravity in the model afterward to recover the inverted dynamics. Designed an LQR feedback controller, with the Q and R matrices sized using Bryson's rule, and a Kalman filter for state estimation, to stabilize the underactuated cart-pendulum, validated against encoder and motor torque data from the real hardware.
